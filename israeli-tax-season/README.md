# Israeli Tax Season Bundle

Tax season in Israel means navigating annual returns, bimonthly VAT reports, withholding reconciliation, and finding every legitimate deduction. This bundle combines five skills that cover the full tax workflow for individuals, freelancers, and businesses.

## Key facts

- **Annual return deadline**: April 30 for individuals, with online extensions typically available until May 31 (Income Tax Ordinance — פקודת מס הכנסה).
- **Annual return form**: Tofes 1301 (individuals) / Tofes 1214 (self-employed attachment).
- **VAT reporting**: bimonthly — every 2 months by the 15th of the following month, filed with Rashut HaMisim.
- **Withholding at source** (ניכוי מס במקור): rates vary by service category and supplier certificate (ishur nikui b'makor). Withheld amounts are reconciled against annual tax liability.
- **Refund window**: you can file to recover overpaid tax **retroactively up to 6 years back** (סעיף 160 לפקודת מס הכנסה).
- **Osek patur threshold** (2026): annual revenue under ~120,000 NIS — exempt from VAT but still files income tax.

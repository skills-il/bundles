Whether you opened a shop last month or have been running your business for years, these skills speak the language of Israeli business -- electronic invoices through the Tax Authority clearance system, bituach leumi rates, and Hebrew customer communication built in from the start.

## Key facts

- **Electronic invoice (Chashbonit Mas) clearance**: for invoices above the clearance threshold, Rashut HaMisim requires a clearance number issued via the Tax Authority's clearance system before the invoice is considered valid.
- **VAT reporting**: bimonthly filing by the 15th of the following month (Value Added Tax Law).
- **Bituach Leumi and employer contributions**: paid monthly via Masav with the 15th of the month as a common deadline.
- **Payroll (tlush maskoret)**: must include mandatory fields — gross, net, income tax, Bituach Leumi, health tax, pension, keren hishtalmut, and cumulative totals.
- **Payment terms law** (חוק מועדי תשלום): standard payer must pay within **45 days** of the invoice end-of-month; the public sector has stricter rules.
- **Record retention**: 7 years for tax and business records (Income Tax Ordinance).

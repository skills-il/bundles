# Olim Chadashim Starter Kit

The first month of aliyah is a blur of bureaucracy. Misrad HaKlita, Misrad HaPnim, Bituach Leumi, kupat cholim selection, opening a bank account, finding an apartment, setting up a cell phone, and figuring out if your foreign driver's license still works. Every new oleh asks the same 5-6 questions and gets 5-6 different answers. This bundle gives you clear, current answers in English and Hebrew, with action checklists you can follow from the airport.

## Who is this for?

- **New olim arriving from anywhere** (North America, UK, France, FSU, South America, Ethiopia, South Africa)
- **Returning Israelis** (toshavim chozrim) who left before 18 and are coming back as adults
- **Family members of olim** who are on a different status track
- **Diaspora planners** researching aliyah 6-12 months ahead who want to know exactly what they're walking into

## What you get

1. **Israeli Aliyah Navigator** — Misrad HaKlita, Sal Klita, absorption basket, rights timeline, document checklist
2. **Israeli HMO Navigator** — pick between Clalit, Maccabi, Meuhedet, and Leumit based on your neighborhood, family size, and health needs
3. **Israeli Bituach Leumi** — registration, entitlements as a new oleh, and the forms that actually matter
4. **Israeli Apartment Hunting** — Yad2, avoiding scams, deposit rules, and neighborhood guide
5. **Israeli Bank Reconciliation** — open an account, understand Israeli banking quirks (Masav, ha'amadot, fees), and reconcile your statements
6. **Israeli Vehicle Manager** — convert your foreign driver's license (the process varies dramatically by country of origin)

## How it works

1. Start with Aliyah Navigator the week before you land — it prints a day-one checklist
2. Pick a kupat cholim within your first two weeks (HMO Navigator)
3. Open a bank account and set up Bituach Leumi in parallel
4. Start apartment hunting seriously after the airport-area hotel stay
5. License conversion is not urgent but has a hard deadline at month 12 — don't forget it

## Why this is different from the Nefesh B'Nefesh guides

NBN guides are excellent for the "should I make aliyah" decision. This bundle is for the "I already landed, now what" reality. It's action-oriented, current, and bilingual, and it assumes you want answers right now, not a 30-page PDF.

# Government Navigator Bundle

Israeli bureaucracy is famously complex. Between government offices, Bituach Leumi, municipal services, and an endless stream of forms, even simple tasks can take hours of research and waiting. This bundle brings together five AI skills that help you navigate the system, understand your rights, and get things done faster.

## Key facts

- **Bituach Leumi appeals** must be filed to the ve'adat irurim within **30 days** of receiving the decision (National Insurance Law — חוק הביטוח הלאומי).
- **Freedom of Information Law** (חוק חופש המידע, 1998): Israeli public bodies must respond to information requests within **30 days**, extendable by another 30 with justification.
- **Gov.il single sign-on** is the unified identity layer for most government services; registration requires a teudat zehut and an active phone number.
- **Parking fines**: municipalities must respond to formal appeals within **90 days** (Traffic Ordinance regulations).
- **Teudat zehut** (biometric blue card): must be renewed periodically; the smart card is issued by Misrad HaPnim (Ministry of Interior).
- **Electronic signatures**: the Electronic Signature Law (חוק חתימה אלקטרונית, 2001) recognizes digital signatures for most civil filings.
